## Purpose

Provides quantitative and qualitative analysis of a meeting's dynamics. This helps team leads understand participation patterns, identify dominant speakers, track engagement over time, evaluate the emotional tone of the discussion, and categorize the topics discussed â€” turning subjective meeting experiences into objective, data-driven insights.

## User Journey

1. The user navigates to a completed meeting's Insights view.
2. The system fetches the transcript and computes analytics.
3. The dashboard displays:
   - **Speaker Statistics**: Word count, speaking time percentage per participant (as a pie/bar chart).
   - **Engagement Timeline**: Activity volume over the meeting's duration (line chart).
   - **Topic Breakdown**: Categories of subjects discussed (grouped bar/pie chart).
   - **Sentiment Score**: Overall emotional tone of the meeting (positive/neutral/negative indicator).
4. The user can explore individual sections to understand meeting dynamics.
## Functional Behaviour

Analytics generation combines two distinct approaches: **pure computation** and **LLM analysis**. Speaker statistics (`calculateSpeakerStats`) count words per speaker across all segments, mapping generic speaker IDs to names using the meeting's participant list. The engagement timeline (`calculateEngagementTimeline`) groups segment activity by minute to produce a time-series data set. These are deterministic computations with no LLM involvement. Separately, the system sends the transcript to the LLM for **sentiment analysis** (scoring the overall emotional tone) and **topic breakdown** (categorizing subjects discussed). The computed and AI-generated results are merged into a single analytics response object. In DEMO_MODE, hardcoded mock analytics data is returned.

## Data Created Or Modified

No persistent entities are created. Analytics are computed on-demand and returned ephemerally (not cached).

## Limitations

- Analytics are recomputed from scratch on every request â€” no caching or persistence.
- Speaker resolution logic is duplicated inside the analytics computation rather than reusing M2's resolution.
- Pure computational logic (`calculateSpeakerStats`) is currently embedded inside `LLMService`, mixing concerns.
- Heavy mock data is hardcoded for DEMO_MODE, which may drift from actual analytics structure over time.
- No cross-meeting analytics (e.g., trends across multiple meetings in a workspace).
